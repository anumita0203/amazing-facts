The pre-frontal cortex is the thinking part of our brain responsible for much of human progress.
The amygdala appeared in the earliest vertebrates around 550 million years ago, and the pre-frontal cortex appeared around 4 million years ago. It's really easy for the amygdala to override the pre-frontal cortex.
Dopamine appeared as a reward mechanism to motivate the organism to work hard for specific rewards because they increased its chances of survival.
